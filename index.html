Master Thesis

Battery Safety Research

Development of a Benchtop Testing Methodology for Thermal Runaway Characterization of Li-Ion Cells

Akkuracy Battery Testing GmbH / Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg

The thesis focused on developing a simplified but accurate benchtop method to study energy release during thermal runaway of large-format lithium-ion cells. The method was designed to provide faster and more cost-effective screening before full calorimetric testing.

  • Developed and implemented a modified benchtop thermal runaway test methodology for large-format lithium-ion cells with calorimetry-level accuracy.
  • Designed and integrated custom instrumentation to measure internal cell core temperature during thermal runaway events.
  • Quantified energy release from cell can, ejecta, and vent gases during abuse testing of lithium-ion cells.
  • Validated the methodology against DATRC calorimetry to evaluate energy capture accuracy and test reliability.
  • Established a rapid pre-calorimetry screening approach for battery safety characterization and thermal runaway evaluation.
