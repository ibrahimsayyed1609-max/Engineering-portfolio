Battery Safety & Thermal Runaway Analysis

Master's Thesis

Development of a Benchtop Testing Methodology for Thermal Runaway Characterization of Li-ion Cells.

Key Responsibilities

  • Developed and implemented a modified benchtop thermal runaway test methodology for large-format lithium-ion cells with calorimetry-level accuracy.
  • Designed and integrated custom instrumentation to measure internal cell core temperature during thermal runaway events.
  • Performed quantitative analysis of energy release from cell can, ejecta, and vent gases during abuse testing of lithium-ion cells.
  • Conducted comparative validation of the developed methodology against DATRC calorimetry to evaluate energy capture accuracy and test reliability.
  • Established a rapid and cost-effective pre-calorimetry screening approach for battery safety characterization and thermal runaway evaluation.
